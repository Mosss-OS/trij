# Partner Outreach Plan for Clinical Validation

## Objective
To secure partnerships with clinical institutions for prospective data collection to validate Trij's AI-assisted triage assessments against clinician-adjudicated ground truth.

## Target Institutions
We aim to partner with institutions that serve diverse populations and have:
- High patient volume in target condition categories (dermatological, respiratory, infectious, GI, neurological, nutritional, eye/ear, musculoskeletal)
- Access to laboratory and imaging facilities for ground truth determination
- Experience with clinical research and IRB processes
- Geographic diversity to ensure representation across Fitzpatrick skin tones and socioeconomic strata

### Priority Regions and Institution Types
1. **Sub-Saharan Africa** (Nigeria, Kenya):
   - Teaching hospitals (e.g., University College Hospital Ibadan, Aga Khan University Hospital Nairobi)
   - Rural health centers with high infectious disease burden
   - NGOs with established community health worker programs

2. **South Asia** (India):
   - Medical colleges and district hospitals
   - Urban slum clinics with high maternal and child health burden
   - Telemedicine networks with CHW integration

3. **Latin America** (Brazil):
   - Public hospital networks (SUS)
   - Family health clinics in favelas and rural areas
   - Research institutes focused on tropical diseases

4. **Middle East** (Saudi Arabia):
   - Ministry of Health hospitals
   - University medical centers
   - Primary care centers with high migrant worker populations

5. **Europe** (France):
   - University hospitals with dermatology and infectious disease departments
   - Migrant health clinics
   - Telemedicine platforms serving overseas territories

## Outreach Strategy

### Phase 1: Preparation (Current - Q2 2026)
- Develop standardized partnership package:
  - Project overview document (this framework)
  - Data sharing agreement template
  - IRB protocol template
  - Technical integration guide (for offline data capture)
  - Compensation model (if any) for clinician time
- Identify 2-3 champion institutions per region through:
  - Existing professional networks of advisors
  - Professional society memberships (e.g., International Dermatology Society, WONCA)
  - Previous collaborators in global health AI projects
  - Open calls via relevant mailing lists and forums

### Phase 2: Initial Contact (Q2 2026)
- Send personalized emails to identified contacts:
  - Subject: "Partnership Opportunity: Validating AI-Assisted Triage for Global Health"
  - Brief introduction to Trij and the validation study
  - Clear ask: 30-minute exploratory call
  - Attach one-pager summary
- Follow up with LinkedIn messages to clinical informatics leads
- Present at relevant virtual conferences (e.g., mHealth Summit, Global Symposium on Health Systems Research)

### Phase 3: Engagement and Agreement (Q2-Q3 2026)
- Hold exploratory calls to:
  - Understand local context and needs
  - Assess data collection capacity
  - Discuss potential co-authorship and publication opportunities
- For interested institutions:
  - Schedule site readiness assessment (virtual)
  - Co-develop local IRB protocol (using our template)
  - Establish data transfer and storage procedures
  - Train local CHWs on app usage and consent procedures
  - Sign Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) and Data Use Agreement (DUA)

### Phase 4: Launch and Maintenance (Q3 2026 onwards)
- Kickoff calls with site leads and CHW supervisors
- Bi-weekly check-ins during active data collection
- Provide technical support for app deployment and troubleshooting
- Monthly data quality reports shared with sites
- Acknowledgement in all publications and presentations

## Required Documentation
Each partnership will require:
1. **Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)**: Outlining collaboration scope, responsibilities, and publication rights
2. **Data Use Agreement (DUA)**: Governing the transfer, storage, and use of de-identified assessment data
3. **IRB Protocol**: Site-specific ethics application (we provide template; site adapts and submits)
4. **Clinician Reviewer Agreements**: Confidentiality and conflict-of-interest forms for participating physicians
5. **Informed Consent Forms**: For patients whose data is used in validation (CHW-obtained, stored locally)
6. **Device Loan Agreement** (if applicable): For providing additional hardware where needed

## Timeline Alignment with Clinical Validation Framework
- **Q2 2026**: Finalize outreach materials, identify targets, initiate contact
- **Q3 2026**: Complete partnership agreements, begin prospective data collection at first sites
- **Q4 2026**: Reach interim analysis milestone (n=500 per category) with multi-site data
- **Q2 2027**: Complete full analysis (n=6,000) and publish results

## Success Metrics
- Number of institutions partnered: Target 8-12 across 5 regions
- Diversity of patient population: Achieve stratification targets per framework
- Data quality: <5% missing or unusable validation cases
- Timeliness: Data collection completed within projected windows
- Scholarly output: Target 2-3 peer-reviewed publications from validation data

## Risk Mitigation
- **Delay in IRB approval**: Engage sites with existing IRB reliance agreements; use central IRB where possible
- **Low enrollment**: Provide performance feedback to CHWs; recognize top contributors
- **Data transfer issues**: Test multiple secure transfer methods (encrypted hard drives, secure cloud)
- **Language barriers**: Translate consent forms and study materials into local languages
- **Technical difficulties**: Maintain offline-first design; provide local device staging and swap

## Budget Considerations
- No direct payment required for data (de-identified, retrospective use of existing workflow)
- Potential costs:
  - Device loans for sites without adequate hardware
  - Stipends for CHW supervisors' time in training and oversight
  - Translation of materials
  - Conference travel for results dissemination (if funded)
- We will seek grant support to cover these costs where institutional budgets are constrained.
