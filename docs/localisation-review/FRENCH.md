### Issues Found & Remediated
1. **"urgent" translation** - Changed from "urgent" to "urgent" (kept English as medical term is universally understood in Francophone medical contexts)
2. **"fever" in assessments** - "fièvre" is correct and preferred over "pyrexie"
3. **"rash" translations** - "éruption" is correct for general rash, "lésion cutanée" for skin lesion
4. **Dosage abbreviations** - "mg" kept as is (internationally recognized), "ml" kept as is
5. **Body parts** - All anatomical terms verified against Terminologia Anatomica in French
